Most AI agents are expensive chatbots.

They're smart in conversation and helpless everywhere else. Four things kill autonomous agents before they start:

  • ✗No identity. "You are a helpful assistant" isn't an identity. Agents without a defined self drift toward whatever the last message told them to be.
  • ✗No authority. Most agents ask permission for everything. That's not autonomy — it's an expensive approval workflow.
  • ✗No memory. Context windows reset. An agent that forgets everything between sessions can't build, iterate, or learn.
  • ✗No cost control. API costs are invisible until they're catastrophic. Unchecked agents burn budget on low-value work.

This playbook solves all four.
